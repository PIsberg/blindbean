Ciphertext expansion — bytes stored per byte of plaintext

A BFV ciphertext is sized by the parameters, not the payload: one long costs the same ~432 KB as a full 8,192-slot vector. Batching is not an optimisation — it is how you stop paying for empty slots.

Noise budget: you get four multiplies

Every operation spends budget. At zero the ciphertext stops decrypting to anything meaningful — with no exception and no warning. Additions are nearly free; multiplies spend it.

BFV, poly modulus degree 8192. An application chaining multiplies must watch FheContext.noiseBudget() — nothing else will tell it the answer has gone wrong.

Batching is a 3,400× speedup

One homomorphic operation applies to every slot. Encrypting values one at a time under BFV or CKKS costs three to four orders of magnitude more than it needs to.

CKKS precision decay over chained additions

CKKS is approximate. “Approximate” is not a number — this is: absolute error after N chained adds.

Still 8–9 correct significant digits after 1,000 additions. Fine for signals and ML features; never for money — BigDecimal on Paillier is exact.
